# OpenClaw Program Reference

## Program

- Name: OpenClaw.
- Brand/teacher: Anh Lap Trinh.
- Main positioning: Help non-technical people create a team of AI assistants that run on their own computer and perform real work after receiving commands.
- Core promise: The learner does not need to know programming first. If they understand their own work, they can create assistants for that work.
- Main slogan/idea: Ra lệnh, AI làm việc trên máy tính thay bạn.
- Audience: freelancers, founders, people preparing to start a business, office workers, operators, and anyone who loves automation and technology.

## Product Concept

- OpenClaw is presented as a system for creating many AI assistants, not a single chatbot.
- Each assistant can own a separate role: schedule reminder, content assistant, Excel assistant, website assistant, customer care assistant, data/reporting assistant, and similar roles.
- The user gives commands through group chat or channels such as Telegram/Zalo by calling the right assistant.
- Each assistant can have its own workspace and work history, so jobs do not get mixed together.
- The assistants run on the user's own computer. The site says OpenClaw supports Windows and macOS.
- The learner may need APIs from OpenAI, Gemini, Claude, or similar AI providers. Cost depends on usage.

## What Learners Build Or Automate

- Create AI assistants on the computer and train/use them for real work.
- Combine OpenClaw with Antigravity to save time and resources.
- Control websites: open browser, log in, click, enter data, fill forms, collect information, download documents.
- Read and process local files: Excel, Word, PDFs, and other documents.
- Analyze data and create reports from files or websites.
- Build workflows for Excel, reporting, customer management, HR, accounting, sales, admin, and operations.
- Create content and images, then post or schedule posts on social platforms.
- Reply to Facebook Fanpage messages and support customer-care workflows.
- Create websites, connect domains, and automate deployment-like tasks when the learner has the required infrastructure.

## Why It Is Different From ChatGPT

- ChatGPT is framed as question-and-answer inside a chat interface.
- OpenClaw is framed as command-and-action: it can control the computer, browser, files, messages, and workflows.
- The important sales bridge: many people already ask AI for answers, but still do the boring clicks, copy/paste, downloads, reports, and checking by themselves. OpenClaw aims to move AI from "answering" to "doing".

## Teaching Method

- The course is positioned as A-to-Z setup and usage guidance for beginners.
- The teaching style is "vướng đâu gỡ đó": start using the tool, get stuck, then solve the exact obstacle.
- It avoids a fixed theory-heavy syllabus in the early stage. The site emphasizes outcomes and adaptation to each learner's work.
- Learners watch videos and can study when they have time.
- Support channels mentioned: UltraViewer, Zalo, Zoom, and direct help when stuck.
- The site repeatedly tells learners the beginning can feel unfamiliar and difficult; they need to practice until they get used to the tool.

## Common Objections And Responses

### "I want the detailed lesson list"

- Answer from the positioning: lesson lists are less important than what the learner can do after learning.
- Emphasize that the program adjusts based on the learner's actual workflow, as long as it creates useful results.

### "I do not know programming or AI"

- Answer: the program starts from setup and usage. The learner follows video guidance and asks for help when stuck.
- Keep the tone calming but direct: most beginners are zero at first; practice makes it familiar.

### "Can it apply to my job?"

- Answer: each job has its own workflow, so ready-made tools often do not fit. The learner creates assistants for their own process because they understand their work best.

### "I am older or weak with technology"

- Answer: age is not the main barrier. The beginning is unfamiliar, but repeated practice matters more.
- Do not make age feel cute or inspirational. Keep it practical and a little blunt.

### "Why is there no trial/demo product?"

- Answer: the assistant needs to be created for the learner's own workflow, not used as someone else's ready-made product.
- Demo videos can show possibilities, but the real value is creating the learner's own assistant.

### "Why is the course paid?"

- Answer: payment creates seriousness and a reason to practice. Avoid sounding defensive; frame it as commitment.

### "Will this be perfect right away?"

- Answer: no. Treat an AI assistant like a new employee. Let it work first, then improve prompts, data, rules, and workflow over time.

## Story Arcs

### Founder Doing Everything Alone

The reader is building a business and doing content, images, fanpage replies, data checking, reports, customer messages, and website tasks by themselves. They do not need another motivational AI post; they need assistants that can actually take repeatable computer work off their hands.

### ChatGPT Still Leaves Manual Work

The reader asks ChatGPT for ideas, scripts, or analysis, but still opens websites, downloads files, copies data, edits Excel, sends messages, and posts content manually. OpenClaw becomes the next step: not just answer, but act.

### Ready-Made Tools Do Not Fit

The reader has tried software, extensions, templates, or no-code tools. They help a bit, but the workflow is too specific. The answer is not always to find another tool; sometimes it is to create a personal assistant that follows the reader's process.

### Perfection Trap

The reader keeps tuning prompts, planning workflows, and wanting a perfect setup before using it. Reframe: make the assistant run first, accept imperfect output, then improve it after real usage.

### Low-Tech Beginner

The reader is afraid of installation screens, API keys, unfamiliar interfaces, or automation concepts. Reframe: everyone is clumsy at first. Follow the video, ask when stuck, repeat until the interface feels normal.

## Proof And Offer Points

- The site presents OpenClaw as a paid program with setup, usage guidance, and practical support.
- The site mentions a 7-day refund guarantee.
- The site lists company identity and contact channels for trust-building.
- The site mentions a fee of 1,480K for the fastest 5 learners "today", with a note that the fee may increase. Treat this as date-sensitive and verify before publishing.

## Date-Sensitive Checks

Before publishing copy that includes price, number of slots, guarantee terms, current bonuses, APIs, platform support, or "today" urgency, verify the latest source page or ask the user to confirm.
