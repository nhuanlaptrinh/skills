# Viet Bai VibePython

## Core Workflow

1. Read `references/program.md` for the course facts, offer, audience, proof points, objections, and calls to action.
2. Read `references/voice.md` before writing any public-facing copy.
3. Choose one story angle before drafting:
   - Office worker stuck with repetitive Excel, Word, PDF, website tasks.
   - Person over 30 who thinks programming is only for smart/young/English-good people.
   - Learner who studied Python elsewhere, got bored, and did not know how to apply it.
   - Employee who wants a visible career breakthrough, extra income, outsourcing, or a custom tool.
   - Business/user who cannot find ready-made software flexible enough for their real work.
4. Draft from the reader's everyday pain, then move into the method: Antigravity AI first for fast results, Python second for understanding, editing, debugging, and flexible projects.
5. End with one clear action: register, message for advice, or start learning immediately.

## Writing Rules

- Write in Vietnamese.
- Prioritize story-driven copy over feature listing.
- Sound like a practical older-brother guide: direct, conversational, sometimes blunt, never polished-corporate.
- Use "anh em" naturally when the target audience is general working adults; use "bạn" when the copy needs to feel more personal or landing-page friendly.
- Keep the main promise practical: create tools/apps for real work, reduce boring manual tasks, and learn by doing.
- Emphasize "thực hành trước, lý thuyết sau" and "vướng đâu, gỡ đó" as the method.
- Keep the two-stage structure clear:
  1. Vibe Coding with Antigravity AI: create app/tool quickly by giving instructions.
  2. Python automation: understand, customize, fix bugs, and build more flexible projects.
- Include realistic work examples: Excel, PDF, Word, websites, email automation, mouse/keyboard automation, data scraping, customer management tools, HR/accounting workflows.
- Mention support only as practical help: Zalo, Zoom, video, UltraViewer, 1:1 guidance when stuck.
- Use proof points only if relevant: 6000+ learners, around 3 years of teaching, many learners are office workers or career switchers, 7-day refund guarantee, transparent company/social channels.
- Do not promise guaranteed income, guaranteed promotion, or effortless success. Frame extra income and career change as possible paths after serious application.

## Output Shapes

For a Facebook/Zalo post:
- Hook with a lived scene or blunt belief.
- Tell a short transformation story.
- Explain the 2-stage method.
- Add proof or objection handling.
- Close with a direct CTA.

For an email:
- Subject line in the same voice, not clickbait-heavy.
- Short opening story.
- Practical lesson or belief shift.
- Course bridge.
- Clear CTA.

For landing page sections:
- Use short sections with direct headings.
- Keep details concrete.
- Avoid startup-style jargon and shiny marketing language.

## Guardrails

- Do not copy long passages from the source website. Preserve the voice by using the style patterns in `voice.md`, not by pasting source paragraphs.
- Avoid sounding like a generic education ad: no "unlock your potential", "master the future", "world-class curriculum", or vague hype.
- Keep humor/bluntness purposeful. Do not overdo profanity or shock lines.
- If the user asks for a very specific channel, adapt length and structure to that channel.
